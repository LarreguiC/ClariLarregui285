What is PCOS?

PCOS stands for Polycystic Ovary Syndrome, and is a common hormonal condition which affects women of reproductive age. This disorder causes an enlarging of the ovaries with small cysts (follicles) developing on the outer edges.

Information About PCOS

PCOS affects women between the ages of 15 to 44. PCOS affects woman's ovaries which produces estrogen and progesterone that helps regulate the menstrual cycle. The ovaries also produce a small amount of male hormones called androgens. Women with PCOS produce a higher amount of the horomone androgens.

With PCOS woman are at greater risk of developing Diabetes, Hypertension, High Cholesterol, Cardiovascular Disease, Stroke, Depression and even Infertility for some women. With a proper diet and exercise the risk factors are reduced significantly.

Types of PCOS

There are four different types of PCOS woman can have.

Insulin-Resistant PCOS

This is the most common type of PCOS. The body does not respond to insulin as quickly as it should. This condition leads to high glucose levels. This can lead to one becoming pre-diabetic which can increase the risk of type 2 diabetes and other metabolic conditions.

Pill-Induced PCOS

This is the second most common type of PCOS. This occurs if one has experienced the symptoms of PCOS after taking contraceptives but prior to this had never expereinced signs of PCOS and had experience regular menstrual and ovulation. Birth control pills supress ovulation so if one were to stop this treatment, one may not be able to ovaulate or have a regular menstrual cycle anymore. These symptoms can be temporary that can lasts for a few months but for some it may not.

Inflammatory PCOS

The ovaries stop ovulating due to factors caused by stress either emotional or mental which contributes to the over excessive amount of androgens, dhea levels and testosterone. Women experience high levels of CRP (C-Reactive Protein.) Certain diets can trigger inflammation such as diary, sugar, alcohol, even gluten.

Hidden PCOS

This type of PCOS can be harder to know if one does have PCOS. A number of factors such as diet, thyroid problems or deficiences such as iron or iodine that affects the ovaries. These factors can cause the hidden PCOS.

Common PCOS Symptoms

PCOS affects a woman's menstrual cycle from irregularity to completely not having it at all. Woman with PCOS develop multiple cysts in the ovaries, as well as experiencing weight gain which can lead to obesity. Other symptoms of PCOS can include

Acne or Oily Skin

Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS) / Acne Inversa which can develop on the groin area, breast even underneath the armpits

Hirsutism (Excessive Hair Growth) this includes the face, chest, back, stomach etc.

Male-Pattern Baldness

Depression and Anxiety

Difficulties getting pregnant/Infertility

PCOS Diet

Woman can experience symptoms differently from others most of the time it is based on the food that is consumed, but thankfully by eating clean and healthy PCOS symptoms can be managed, controlled and even reduced.

Foods to Eat


Natural unprocessed foods

Low Fat Foods

Leafy Greens (Kale, Spinach, etc.)

Lean Proteins

Fatty Fish (Salmon, Tuna, Sardines, Mackerel

High Fiber foods

Dried beans, lentils, other legumes

Healthy Fats (Olive Oil, Coconut Oil/Coconuts, Almonds, Avocados

Nuts (Almonds, Cashews, Walnuts etc.

Fruits (Strawberries, Blueberries, Raspberries)

Foods to Avoid


Refined carbohydrates (White breads, Pasta, White Rice or pastries)

Fried Foods & Fast Foods

Processed meats

Sugary Beverages (Sodas, Juices, Energy Drinks)

Excessive Amounts of Red meats (Pork, Hamburgers, Steaks)

Artifical or heavily processed chesses

Dairy (in some cases) can cause inflammation (Milk, Yogurts, Cheese etc.)

Limit Alcohol intake
